Ingredients

  • 2 bananas, very ripe
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup flour
  • cinnamon

Momrev's Jamaican Banana Bread

Before there were Facebook walls...

There were the walls of a villa in Kingston, Jamaica, where my mother was vacationing with her parents in 1973. The cook -- who made banana bread every day -- had written her recipe on the wall of the kitchen in black magic marker. Young Momrev copied it down, brought it home, and made it probably hundreds of times in the course of my childhood. I still throw it together every time I have two overripe bananas kicking around. Easy and delightful.


Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, mash bananas and soda with a fork.
  2. Add sugar, oil, and egg, and stir well. Add flour and a few shakes of cinnamon.
  3. Grease a loaf pan (with butter, vegetable oil or spray oil like Pam) and pour in the batter.
  4. Bake for about 45 minutes at 350F. Watch the cake near the end of the baking time so the top of the cake does not get too dark.
